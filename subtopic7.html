Basic Financial Terms

Understand the fundamental vocabulary of personal finance and investing.

💡 Click on cards to see full details and examples.

💰

Assets

Anything of economic value owned by an individual or company that can be converted into cash. Assets can be current (liquid, short-term) or fixed (long-term, illiquid).

Details

  • Current Assets: Cash, savings accounts, inventory, accounts receivable.
  • Fixed Assets: Real estate, machinery, equipment, long-term investments.
  • Assets are listed on the balance sheet and represent what a company or individual owns.
  • Understanding your assets is crucial for calculating net worth.
💸

Liabilities

Financial obligations or debts owed to another party. Liabilities represent what a company or individual owes. They can be current (short-term) or long-term.

Details

  • **Current Liabilities:** Accounts payable, short-term loans, utility bills.
  • **Long-term Liabilities:** Mortgages, car loans, student loans, bonds payable.
  • Liabilities are also listed on the balance sheet.
  • Managing liabilities is key to financial health and avoiding excessive debt.
📊

Net Worth

The value of all financial and non-financial assets owned, minus the value of all outstanding liabilities. It's a key indicator of an individual's or company's financial health.

Details

  • Calculated as: Assets - Liabilities = Net Worth.
  • A positive net worth indicates more assets than debts.
  • A negative net worth indicates more debts than assets.
  • Tracking net worth over time helps assess financial progress.
📈

Interest

The cost of borrowing money or the return on lending money. It is typically expressed as a percentage of the principal amount. Can be simple or compound.

Details

  • **Simple Interest:** Calculated only on the principal amount. \$I = P \times R \times T\$
  • **Compound Interest:** Calculated on the principal amount and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods. This is often called "interest on interest."
  • Understanding interest is vital for loans, savings, and investments.
  • High interest rates on debt can significantly increase repayment costs.
🛡️

Inflation

The rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising, and subsequently, purchasing power is falling. It erodes the value of money over time.

Details

  • Measured by indices like the Consumer Price Index (CPI).
  • High inflation reduces the real return on savings and investments.
  • Investors often seek assets that can outpace inflation to preserve purchasing power.
  • Central banks often manage interest rates to control inflation.
💰

Budget

A financial plan that estimates an individual's or company's income and expenses over a specific future period. It helps in managing money effectively and achieving financial goals.

Details

  • Helps track where money comes from and where it goes.
  • Essential for identifying areas to save and reduce unnecessary spending.
  • Common budgeting methods include the 50/30/20 rule (Needs/Wants/Savings).
  • Regularly reviewing and adjusting your budget is crucial.
🏦

Credit Score

A numerical expression based on a level analysis of a person's credit files, to represent the creditworthiness of an individual. Lenders use it to assess risk.

Details

  • Ranges typically from 300-850 (e.g., FICO score).
  • Higher scores indicate lower risk and can lead to better loan terms.
  • Factors include payment history, amounts owed, length of credit history, new credit, and credit mix.
  • Regularly checking your credit report for errors is important.

Key Financial Concepts

✅
Income: Money received, especially on a regular basis, for work or through investments.
✅
Expenses: The cost required for something; money spent on something.
✅
Savings: Money set aside for future use, typically in a bank account.
✅
Investment: An asset or item acquired with the goal of generating income or appreciation.
✅
Diversification: Spreading investments to reduce risk.
✅
Risk Tolerance: An investor's willingness to take on financial risk.
✅
Liquidity: The ease with which an asset can be converted into cash.
