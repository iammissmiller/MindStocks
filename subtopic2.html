What is SEBI?

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) is the primary regulator of the Indian securities market. It ensures fair, transparent, and efficient trading while safeguarding the interests of investors. SEBI sets the rules that all market participants must follow. (The referee of the Indian securities market)

Details

  • Ensures the stock market works fairly and transparently.
  • Protects investors from fraud, scams, and insider trading.
  • Regulates and develops the securities market in India.

Protecting Investors

SEBI works to ensure that investors receive accurate and timely information from companies before making decisions. It actively prevents fraud, insider trading, and malpractices that could harm investors. This builds trust and confidence in the market.

Details

  • Ensures companies share correct financial reports.
  • Prevents brokers from overcharging or misusing funds.
  • Stops misuse of secret company information.

Regulating the Market

SEBI creates and enforces regulations for companies, stock exchanges, and brokers to maintain order in the market. It monitors trading activities to ensure they comply with laws. By doing so, it prevents unfair advantages and market manipulation.

Details

  • Ensures trading happens legally and efficiently.
  • Oversees compliance with market regulations.
  • Monitors market behavior for irregularities.

Developing the Market

SEBI ensures that all market participants follow ethical and professional standards. It takes action against entities involved in malpractice or violations. This helps maintain market integrity and investor trust.

Dispute Resolution

SEBI provides mechanisms for resolving complaints between investors and market intermediaries. It ensures quick and fair settlements to avoid prolonged disputes. This gives investors a sense of safety and reliability in the market.

Details

  • Provides a platform for investor complaints.
  • Ensures quick and fair settlements.
  • Prevents prolonged market disputes.
