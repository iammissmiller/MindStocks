Right to Information

Investors have the right to receive accurate, timely, and complete information about investments, including financial statements, prospectuses, and risk disclosures.

Details

  • Access to company annual reports and quarterly filings.
  • Clear disclosure of fees, charges, and potential conflicts of interest.
  • Information on the investment's performance history and objectives.
  • Right to receive trade confirmations and account statements.

Right to Fair Treatment

Investors should be treated fairly and ethically by financial professionals and institutions, without discrimination or deceptive practices.

Details

  • Protection against fraud, manipulation, and insider trading.
  • Adherence to "best execution" principles for trades.
  • Fair allocation of investment opportunities.
  • Right to privacy of personal and financial data.

Right to Redress

If an investor suffers losses due to misconduct, fraud, or negligence, they have the right to seek compensation through established legal and regulatory channels.

Details

  • Access to arbitration or mediation services for dispute resolution.
  • Ability to file complaints with regulatory bodies (e.g., SEC, FINRA).
  • Right to pursue legal action against fraudulent parties.
  • Timely and fair resolution of complaints.

Right to Vote (Shareholders)

Shareholders have the right to vote on important company matters, such as electing board members, approving mergers, and other corporate actions.

Details

  • Participation in annual general meetings (AGMs).
  • Voting on executive compensation and major policy changes.
  • Ability to submit shareholder proposals.
  • Proxy voting if unable to attend meetings in person.
üîí

Right to Privacy

Investors have the right to expect that their personal and financial information will be kept confidential and protected from unauthorized access or disclosure.

Details

  • Protection of account details and transaction history.
  • Secure handling of personal identification information.
  • Clear policies on data sharing with third parties.
  • Notification in case of data breaches.

Responsibility to Be Informed

Investors have a responsibility to educate themselves about investments, understand risks, and read all provided documentation before investing.

Details

  • Read prospectuses, offering circulars, and financial reports.
  • Understand the fees, charges, and tax implications.
  • Research the background and credentials of financial professionals.
  • Stay updated on market conditions and economic trends.
üéØ

Responsibility to Set Realistic Goals

Investors should define clear, realistic financial goals and understand that all investments carry some level of risk, with no guarantees of returns.

Details

  • Align investments with personal risk tolerance and time horizon.
  • Avoid chasing unrealistic returns or "get rich quick" schemes.
  • Understand the potential for capital loss.
  • Regularly review and adjust goals as circumstances change.
