Market manipulation (false hype)

People push fake news or coordinated buying to raise a price so insiders can sell at a profit.

Market manipulation (false hype)

Market manipulation happens when scammers spread fake news, rumors, or create hype to artificially drive up a stock’s price. Once ordinary investors jump in, the manipulators sell off their shares, causing the price to collapse and leaving others with losses.

Why it works

People often act on FOMO — the fear of missing out. When prices rise quickly, many rush to buy without verifying the facts, making them easy targets for manipulators.

Example:

A small company is said to have a “big deal” on social apps — price jumps, then collapses after denial.

Quick Tips
  • Wait for official company filings.
  • Check trading volume with real news.
  • Limit buying on hot tips.
Steps
  • Verify news with SEBI/BSE/NSE.
  • Read official statements carefully.
  • Keep a record of trusted sources.
Key Takeaways
  • Don’t act on anonymous tips.
  • Don’t invest without research.
  • Don’t panic buy during hype.

Fake or unregistered advisors / signal groups

Anonymous groups or people sell "premium tips" but may not be legit or registered.

Fake or unregistered advisors / signal groups

Many scammers pose as financial experts, running signal groups or advisory channels without any official registration. They lure investors by showing fake profit screenshots or promising huge returns, but in reality they operate illegally and often disappear with money.

Why it works

Beginners often trust flashy profit screenshots or bold claims, believing that others have already verified the advisor’s credibility.

Example:

A Telegram channel posts edited profit screenshots to attract paid subscribers who then follow risky or fake trading signals.

Quick Tips
  • Always verify SEBI’s official list for licensed advisors.
  • Ask for registration numbers and check their validity online.
  • Test tips in a demo account before risking real money.
Steps
  • Check the advisor’s SEBI registration number.
  • Search for real client reviews on trusted forums.
  • Never share account login details or personal info.
Key Takeaways
  • Don’t pay for tips without proof of consistent performance.
  • Don’t transfer money directly to individuals.
  • Don’t give remote access to your trading account.

High-yield programs / Ponzi-style

Promises of fixed, high returns paid from new investors — not real profits.

High-yield programs / Ponzi-style

Ponzi-style scams promise unusually high and steady returns. Instead of generating real profits, they use money from new investors to pay earlier ones. Eventually, when new deposits slow down, withdrawals are paused and the scheme collapses, leaving most investors at a loss.

Why it works

People are tempted by the idea of guaranteed profits, especially when shown fake testimonials, staged photos, or claims of “happy investors” that make the scheme look trustworthy.

Example:

A website promises 2% daily returns on crypto deposits. Investors are paid at first, but later withdrawals are delayed, and finally the site disappears.

Quick Tips
  • If returns are guaranteed and high, be suspicious — legitimate markets always carry risk.
  • Check for regulation and verified audits before investing.
  • Avoid platforms that do not disclose where returns come from.
Steps
  • Research the business model and revenue sources carefully.
  • Verify third-party audits and legal registrations.
  • Keep track of red flags like withdrawal delays or excuses.
Key Takeaways
  • Don’t invest just because friends or family are involved.
  • Don’t ignore red flags such as delayed payments.
  • Don’t fall for promises of “guaranteed” high returns.

Deepfake / impersonation scams

Fake videos or cloned voices impersonate trusted people to promote scams.

Deepfake / impersonation scams

Scammers use AI-generated videos or voice clones to impersonate famous investors, company executives, or even friends. These deepfakes appear highly convincing and are used to trick people into making payments or investments that go straight to fraudsters.

Why it works

Deepfake technology can mimic voices and faces so accurately that even cautious viewers might be fooled. When these fake endorsements spread through trusted contacts or social media, people often lower their guard.

Example:

A fake video shows a well-known investor endorsing a private fund. Many people invest before the video is debunked as a scam.

Quick Tips
  • Verify endorsements only on official websites or verified social accounts.
  • Look closely for unusual video glitches, voice artifacts, or mismatched lip-syncing.
  • Never click investment links from ads or forwarded messages.
Steps
  • Confirm claims through official company or personality channels.
  • Follow only verified accounts of financial experts.
  • Be skeptical of sensational investment claims in viral media.
Key Takeaways
  • Don’t trust investment advice from ads or forwarded videos.
  • Don’t assume something is real just because it looks convincing.
  • Always verify through trusted, official sources.

Fake “safe” offers (FD/bond receipts)

Scammers offer slightly higher rates on "safe" products using forged documents or fake agents.

Fake “safe” offers (FD/bond receipts)

Scammers exploit the trust people place in fixed deposits and bonds by offering fake schemes with slightly higher interest rates. They use forged receipts, fake agents, or unauthorized paperwork to lure investors. Once money is deposited, withdrawals fail, and the fraudsters vanish with the funds.

Why it works

Fixed deposits and bonds are widely seen as safe, low-risk investments. When scammers offer just a little higher interest rate, it feels like a no-risk upgrade, lowering suspicion and making people more likely to fall for it.

Example:

An “agent” shows forged bank receipts for a special FD scheme. Investors deposit money, but later find that withdrawals are blocked and the receipts were fake.

Quick Tips
  • Invest only through official bank channels or verified brokers.
  • Confirm any certificate or receipt number directly with the bank’s helpline.
  • Never transfer money to a personal account for institutional products.
Steps
  • Check official interest rates on the bank’s official website.
  • Complete all investment paperwork at the bank branch, not outside.
  • Verify documents for official seals, reference numbers, and authenticity.
Key Takeaways
  • Don’t hand over cash or transfer to personal accounts claiming to represent banks.
  • Don’t trust documents without official verification or seals.
  • Don’t fall for “slightly higher” safe returns without thorough checks.

Hyped themed funds / exotic products

Products with fancy names (AI, EV) may hide unrelated assets, high fees, or low liquidity.

Hyped themed funds / exotic products

These products use trendy themes like “AI,” “EV,” or “Green Energy” to attract investors. However, the actual holdings may include unrelated or low-quality assets, often with very high fees or poor liquidity. The fancy name hides the fact that the fund may underperform or carry risks far greater than advertised.

Why it works

Investors don’t want to miss out on “the next big thing.” Clever naming makes these funds sound like a guaranteed ticket to growth, even if the underlying assets don’t match the hype.

Example:

An “AI ETF” claimed to invest in leading AI companies, but actually held unrelated small-cap stocks with high fees, draining investors’ returns.

Quick Tips
  • Read the full fund prospectus and check actual holdings, not just marketing claims.
  • Compare fees and past performance with similar funds and benchmarks.
  • Avoid products that don’t clearly disclose their portfolio.
Steps
  • Look for independent reviews and unbiased expert analysis.
  • Verify that the holdings align with the advertised theme.
  • Check liquidity and ensure the fund can be exited easily if needed.
Key Takeaways
  • Don’t invest just because a theme is trending on social media.
  • Don’t skip reading the fine print in fund disclosures.
  • Don’t assume a flashy name means strong returns.
